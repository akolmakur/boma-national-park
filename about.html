About Boma National Park

Located in the eastern region of South Sudan near the Ethiopian border, Boma National Park spans over 22,800 square kilometers, making it one of the largest protected areas in Africa. Established in 1977, the park plays a vital role in conserving wildlife and ecosystems unique to the region.

Our Mission

To conserve and protect the rich biodiversity of Boma National Park while promoting responsible tourism, environmental education, and community engagement for future generations.

Our Vision

A thriving ecosystem where wildlife and local communities coexist in harmony, and Boma National Park becomes a global destination for eco-tourism and conservation.

Our Core Values

  • Conservation First: Protecting wildlife, ecosystems, and natural resources.
  • Community Involvement: Working hand in hand with local communities.
  • Education & Awareness: Inspiring visitors to value nature.
  • Sustainability: Committed to eco-friendly practices.
  • Adventure with Purpose: Creating experiences that support conservation and growth.
